## Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end HR Workforce Intelligence dashboard developed in Power BI using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance dataset. The dashboard analyzes workforce composition, employee lifecycle, employee experience, and attrition drivers to support data-driven HR decision-making.

The report follows a Workforce Intelligence framework to help HR teams understand workforce trends, identify retention risks, and develop strategic workforce planning initiatives.

---

# Business Objectives

This dashboard aims to answer the following business questions:

- Who makes up our workforce?
- How is the workforce distributed across departments and job roles?
- How are employees progressing through their careers?
- What factors influence employee experience?
- Which workforce segments are at higher risk of attrition?
- How can HR improve employee retention?

---

# Dashboard Overview

The report consists of three interactive dashboard pages.

## Page 1: Employee Demographics & Organizational Structure

Provides an overview of workforce composition and organizational distribution.

### KPIs

- Total Workforce
- Attrition Rate
- Retention Rate
- Average Tenure
- Engagement Index

### Visuals & Insights

#### Department Headcount Distribution
**Purpose**
- Compare workforce size across departments.

**Insight**
- Research & Development employs **961 employees (65.4%)**, followed by Sales (**446; 30.3%**) and Human Resources (**63; 4.3%**), indicating a workforce concentrated in technical functions.

---

#### Average Monthly Income by Department
**Purpose**
- Compare average employee income across departments.

**Insight**
- Sales employees receive the highest average monthly income, followed by Human Resources and Research & Development.

---

#### Employee Distribution by Job Role
**Purpose**
- Identify dominant operational job roles.

**Insight**
- Sales Executive (**326**), Research Scientist (**292**), and Laboratory Technician (**259**) together represent approximately **60%** of the workforce.

---

#### Employee Age Distribution
**Purpose**
- Understand workforce age demographics.

**Insight**
- Most employees belong to the **30–40 years** age group, indicating a predominantly mid-career workforce.

---

#### Educational Profile by Job Role
**Purpose**
- Analyze the educational background of the organization's workforce.

**Insight**
- Employees from **Life Sciences (606; 41.2%) and Medical (464; 31.6%)** backgrounds account for nearly **73%** of the workforce, indicating the organization primarily employs talent from science and healthcare-related disciplines..

---

# Page 2: Career Progression & Employee Experience

Analyzes employee development, learning, and workplace experience.

### KPIs

- Average Monthly Income
- Average Years in Current Role
- Average Training Sessions

### Visuals & Insights

#### Job Level Distribution
**Purpose**
- Understand workforce hierarchy.

**Insight**
- Entry and Associate employees form the largest workforce segment, highlighting opportunities for structured career development.

---

#### Employee Tenure Distribution
**Purpose**
- Analyze workforce maturity using tenure bands.

**Insight**
- Most employees belong to the Established and Veteran tenure categories, resulting in an average organizational tenure of **7.01 years**.

---

#### Training Sessions Last Year
**Purpose**
- Evaluate employee participation in learning initiatives.

**Insight**
- Employees completed an average of **2.8 training sessions** during the previous year, reflecting continuous learning and development.

---

#### Performance Rating Distribution
**Purpose**
- Assess the distribution of employee performance ratings across the workforce.

**Insight**
- Approximately 1.24K employees (84%) received Low performance ratings, while 0.23K employees (16%) received High performance ratings, indicating that most employees fall within the lower performance rating category.

---

#### Job Satisfaction vs Attrition

**Purpose**
- Assess how employee job satisfaction relates to attrition.

**Insight**
- Attrition declines from **22.84%** among employees with the lowest job satisfaction to **11.33%** among those with the highest satisfaction, indicating a strong relationship between satisfaction and retention.

---

#### Work-Life Balance vs Attrition

**Purpose**
- Evaluate the impact of work-life balance on employee retention.

**Insight**
- Employees reporting the lowest work-life balance experience the highest attrition (**31.25%**), compared with **14.22%** among employees with better work-life balance.

---

#### Environment Satisfaction vs Attrition

**Purpose**
- Analyze the relationship between workplace environment satisfaction and employee turnover.

**Insight**
- Attrition decreases from **25.35%** for employees with the lowest environment satisfaction to **13.45%** for those with the highest satisfaction, highlighting the importance of a positive work environment.
---

# Page 3: Attrition Drivers & Workforce Risk Assessment

Identifies workforce risks and factors associated with employee attrition.

### KPIs

- Attrition Count
- Overtime Rate
- Average Years Since Promotion

### Visuals & Insights

#### Department-wise Attrition Rate
**Purpose**
- Identify departments with higher employee turnover.

**Insight**
- Sales records the highest attrition rate (**20.63%**), followed by Human Resources (**19.05%**) and Research & Development (**13.84%**).

---

#### Income Band vs Attrition
**Purpose**
- Analyze attrition across salary bands.

**Insight**
- Employee turnover is concentrated within the **₹2K–₹6K** income band and decreases across higher salary bands.

---

#### Average Experience (Stayed vs Left)
**Purpose**
- Compare organizational experience between retained and exited employees.

**Insight**
- Employees who remained averaged **11.86 years** of experience compared with **8.24 years** among employees who left, representing a **44% higher experience level**.

---

#### Years Since Promotion Distribution
**Purpose**
- Examine promotion patterns across employees.

**Insight**
- Most employees received promotions within the last **5 years**, while relatively few employees experienced prolonged promotion delays.

---

#### Overtime vs Attrition
**Purpose**
- Analyze the relationship between overtime and employee turnover.

**Insight**
- Employees working overtime demonstrate noticeably higher attrition, suggesting workload may contribute to retention challenges.

---

# Executive Insights

- Overall attrition stands at **16.12% (237 employees)**.
- Sales records the highest attrition (**20.63%**), making it the primary focus for retention initiatives.
- Research & Development represents **65.4%** of the workforce (**961 employees**), forming the organization's largest talent pool.
- Employees who remained with the organization averaged **44% more experience** (**11.86 vs 8.24 years**) than employees who left.
- Employee turnover is concentrated within the **₹2K–₹6K** income band, indicating an opportunity to review compensation and retention strategies.
